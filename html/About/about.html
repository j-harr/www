Biking

I grew up in a family that appreciated bike rides on regional trail systems. I have a Trek bike. I enjoy long bikes rides especially in early spring or late fall. I haven't ridden much recently because of my busy schedule.

Driving

I love to drive my car. I learned to drive on a manual, and I have always felt like driving a manual is therapeutic. I appreciate that it takes my mind off of things and helps me to relax. I have gone on many long spontaneous road trips, like to Denver for instance. To save money, I avoid hotels and sleep at rest stops if I need to.

Crocheting

I enjoy crocheting. It is a skill my grandmother taught me when I was younger, and I think it is relaxing and rewarding to be able to see a project take shape and eventually reach completion.

Music

Music plays an active part in my everyday life. I am rarely not listening to music, and when I am in silence, I consider that music in and of itself. I played the trombone and tuba throughout high school. It was nice getting to play whole, half, and quarter notes and worry about my tone instead of worrying about hitting all of the notes.

Horticulture

I'm no plant expert, but I do enjoy plants, flowers, and trees. I can identity a healthy number of trees, and I have a plant of my own that I have been caring for for several years. My favorite types of flowers are the fragant ones that bloom in the evening. Moonflowers come to mind.

Gophers

Since 2015, I have lived among gophers. I named my first groundhog George because he was a solitary fellow. This led me to assume he was a male. George also starts with a G. I understand that isn't an overly deep reason for that name and I apologize. The house I recently moved into has another groundhog. I do not know the gender of the groundhog, and I will likely not be able to know until the month of February. Because of this, I have named he/she February - Febbie or Feb for short. I have a feeling that February is a female, and hopefully I can confirm this in February of 2018.
